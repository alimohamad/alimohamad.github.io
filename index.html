Hi, I'm Ali! I'm a sophomore at Rutgers University majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Creative Writing, a Coach for Major League Hacking, an advocate for intersectionality in tech activism, and an enthusiast of tech intersecting with the arts.


Here's what I've done:

internships usacs hackathons side projects creative projects


tl;dr:

  • In 2016, I organized a data science hackathon for high school students in NYC in collaboration with NASA's Office of Innovation called Space Apps Next Generation: NYC.

  • In 2017, I won 3rd Place Overall at PennApps XV! My team and I built a desktop accessibility tool for Parkinson's disease patients called TactEYEle with IBM Watson's Speech-To-Text API and OpenCV.

  • In 2018, I became Rutgers's Undergraduate Student Alliance of Computer Science's (USACS) Events Coordinator, organizing events to demonstrate the organization's pillars of community, mentorship, diversity and inclusitivity, and hacker culture.

  • In summer of 2018, I interned for Pearson's Learning Services Technology department, working on their Realize team to build an internal developer tool with Java and Python.

  • In 2019, I became a Coach for Major League Hacking. Now I travel the country to empower student hackers and organizers at their events!
